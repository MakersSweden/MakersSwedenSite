Vad är MakersAcademy?

MakersAcademy är en 12 veckor lång, heltidsprogram inom webbutveckling. Makers Academy har sedan starten med stor framgång utbildat över 300 deltagare. Från centrala London's Silicon Roundabout med Storbritanines främsta teknikföretag, är det nu dax för Makers Academy Göteborg att ta emot sin första kull.

Den 22 juni startar vi den första omgången av MakersAcademy Göteborg i samarbete med Makers Academy i London.

Med erfarna coacher tar du dig igenom ett intensivt utbildningsprogram som tar dig från grunderna i programmering till avancerad systemutveckling.

What you'll learn

Ruby & Ruby on Rails

You'll build advanced web applications using the Ruby Language, as well as Ruby on Rails, a powerful web development framework.

HTML5 & CSS3

The fundamentals are always important — we'll show you how to structure web pages and make things look slick.

Agile & Lean & Best-Practise

You'll learn modern lean startup principles & agile methodologies used in software development, as well as best practises like pairing, daily standups, kanban & developing in teams.

JavaScript, jQuery and Node.js

Every web application needs a front-end and javascript lets you build dynamic, full-stack web applications.

Git & Heroku

What if you ruin your code and can't figure out how to fix it? Revert back to previous versions with Git, and push to Heroku to get your website live.

Software Design

From Model-View-Controller to Test Driven Development, we'll show you how to write beautiful software that works, using all the best practises - the kind you can't teach yourself.

Projektarbeten

Hiring Week

Full Time – Like the students who attend our real-time, face-to-face school, you'll be working full time, because this is by far the best way to learn to code. You'll be expected to be at your desk from at least 9am-6pm, Monday-Friday, for the duration of the 12 week course, spending the day pairing with your partner and attending daily standups at 9:30am and 2:30pm. We also have a 4 week Pre-Course, which can be done part-time. (At least 20 hours per week, preferably much more.)

Project-based learning – While the first half of the course focuses on the fundamentals of web development, the second half focuses heavily on students creating real-life projects for their portfolio. You'll leave the course with at least three completed projects - a great asset to show a potential employer. You'll also get to work on team projects, and we'll even offer you experience delivering work for a client, mimicking the work you'll be doing when you start your life as a developer.

More than just syntax – Makers Academy is at the head of a global movement in the software industry. Gone are the days when engineers were locked in a room and told to thoughtlessly bash out thousands of lines of ugly code. Nowadays, the best engineers are artisans, writing Test-Driven, beautiful, readable code. It's next-to-impossible to teach yourself in this way; those who achieve such heights spend years honing their technique. Our students learn to code this way from the day 1, which is why so many awesome companies queue round the block to hire them.

Pair Programming – There is a tired stereotype of the introverted developer, locked in his room with the curtains closed, surrounded by cats and stale pizza. That stereotype is, thankfully, confined to the history books. Nowadays the best developers are also great communicators, and there is no better way to do this than to pair programme every day, constantly discussing and debating your code. At Makers, pairing is right at the heart of our model, and you'll be a pro by the time you graduate
